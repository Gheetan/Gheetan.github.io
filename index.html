Fun Facts

Gheetan's national anthem "The Gheetan Loth" in the Sindarin version can be interpreted as a symbolic representation of the nation’s strength, unity, or essence. In Sindarin, "Loth" could suggest a flower or a bloom, which is often used metaphorically to signify something precious, beautiful, or strong. Thus, "Gheetan Loth" could evoke the image of the flourishing or growth of the Great Gheetan Empire, symbolizing both beauty and strength.

Gheetan's second script is Tengwar.

The Gheetan Loth

Tengwar Script

From hills of green, where rivers sing,
Our banners rise to greet the spring.
With hearts unbowed, we face the light,
A future bright, our hopes take flight.

With steadfast hearts, we stand so tall,
United strong, we shall not fall.
Our Emperor, both wise and bold,
Shall guard our home as days unfold.

But time moves on, the winds grow still,
The empire’s flame begins to chill.
The halls remain, though shadows fall,
And silent whispers fill the wall.
